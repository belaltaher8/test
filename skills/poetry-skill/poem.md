# A Poem

The morning light breaks soft and slow,
Across the fields where wildflowers grow,
A gentle breeze stirs leaf and stem,
The world awakens once again.

The river hums its quiet tune,
Beneath the pale and watching moon,
Each ripple whispers to the shore,
Of things that were and things in store.

The stars retreat at break of day,
Yet leave their light to find a way,
Through clouds and time and winding years,
Through laughter, loss, and joyful tears.

So here we stand, between the vast
And open sky, the future, past,
Each moment bright, each moment brief—
A single, shimmering, golden leaf.
