# A Poem

The morning light breaks soft and slow,
Across the hills where wildflowers grow,
The river hums a silver song,
And time drifts gently all day long.

The trees stand tall in patient grace,
Their roots entangled, intertwined in place,
Their leaves whisper secrets to the breeze,
Of storms endured and passing ease.

The stars emerge when daylight fades,
They scatter light through evening shades,
Each one a story, old and bright,
A quiet flame against the night.

And so we live our fleeting days,
Like sparks that flicker, burn, and blaze,
We reach for something we can't name,
And leave the world not quite the same.
