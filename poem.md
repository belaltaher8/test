# A Poem for 23:08

The clock strikes near the midnight hour,  
Twenty-three past eight, the witching power,  
Tuesday fades, the day draws thin,  
As March's cold breath settles in.  

The world has quieted, stars grow bright,  
A Tuesday slipping into night,  
The city hums a softer tune,  
Beneath a pale and watching moon.  

At twenty-three, the dreamers stir,  
The restless mind begins to blur,  
Between the done and yet-to-be,  
This moment drifts, alive and free.  

So here we pause, at twenty-three-eight,  
Before the midnight comes again,  
One breath, one beat, one fleeting gleam—  
The hour poised between and dream.
