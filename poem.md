# A Poem

The morning light breaks soft and slow,
Across the hills where wildflowers grow,
The wind hums low through ancient trees,
And carries dreams on gentle breeze.

The rivers run with quiet song,
Through valleys wide and shadows long,
Each ripple whispers to the stone,
No wandering heart is ever alone.

The stars above keep faithful watch,
Through summer heat and winter's frost,
They map the sky with silver thread,
A quilt of light above our heads.

So rest, dear soul, and breathe it in—
The world is wide, and joy begins
In moments small and skies so blue,
In everything that makes you, you.
